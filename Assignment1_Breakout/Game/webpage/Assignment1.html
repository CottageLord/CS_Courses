Game Introduction

Rules:

  1. Movement: Press A or D to move the paddle
  2. Win/lose conditions: Player wins the game if all levels' bricks are cleared before losing all 3 lives, and loses if not
  3. Ball movement: The ball bounces on paddle, wall and bricks. The bouncing angle is affected when interacting with paddle, on which different part (left, middle and right) rebounce balls in respective angles
  4. Levels: The game currently have 3 levels, for more information, please see game demonstration section

Collision check

The collision check method is adapted from the Separating Axis Theorem (SAT) in Austin Morlan 's tutorial.

Also, I used an original idea for determing which side doesn the ball hit the brick (though I am quite sure another genius had this idea long before). Here is the demonstration:

When the ball collide with the brick/paddle, it is hard to tell which side does the ball collide on. In the example and previous assignments, we assumed that collision only happen on horizontal/vertical surfaces, hence didn't need such check.

My idea is, given a box-shape collider, calculate the x and y difference with the in coming object, and calculate that ratio. If the abs[(delta y)/(delta x)] ratio is larger than the box-shape collider's h/w, the ball collide on horizontal surface, and vise versa.

I used double for this calculation specifically to ensure precision. However, the behaviour is still not 100% stable. Finding a better collision check (involving vector calculations) could be a explorable improvement in the future.
